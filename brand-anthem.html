A Manifesto Film Script
Brand Film Script
"Theory."
Runtime: 90 seconds
Format: 16:9 Cinematic
Tone: Quiet confidence. Earned, not declared.
Music: Ambient piano. Builds to crescendo.
Scene 1 — Before Dawn
Black screen. The low hum of a machine warming up. A single light turns on — the roastery, 4:47 AM. Steam rises. We see hands adjusting a dial. Close on the roast date: today.
Voiceover
Every morning starts the same way.
Before the doors open.
Before the first order.
Before anyone is watching.
Scene 2 — The Farms
Cut to: Coffee cherries on the branch. Hands picking. A hillside in Ethiopia. Then Colombia. Then Honduras. Quick cuts — vivid, warm, human. End on a cupping table: someone breaking the crust.
Voiceover
We start at the source.
Not a warehouse. Not a catalog.
A farm. A face. A handshake.
Scene 3 — The Roast
The roaster drum turning. Green beans cascading in. The crack — first, then second. Sam pulls a sample, holds it to the light. Nods once. Cut to: beans cooling on the tray, steam billowing.
Voiceover
We don't roast by schedule.
We roast by ear. By color.
By the way the bean tells us it's ready.
Good coffee starts
at the farm.
Scene 4 — The Cafe
California Street flagship. Morning light through the windows. The espresso machine hissing. A barista's hands — tamping, pulling the shot. The pour: amber crema settling. Hand it across the counter. The customer takes a sip. Holds it. Smiles.
Voiceover
This is where it lands.
Not in a boardroom. Not on a slide deck.
In someone's hands. In the first sip of their day.
Scene 5 — The Community
Quick montage: A regular waves from the door. A barista writes a name on a cup — spelled right. Two people share a table, talking, laughing. The parking lot at 7 AM, already full. A kid hands Sam a drawing of a coffee cup.
Voiceover
We're not building a brand.
We're building a place.
A reason to come back.
Scene 6 — The Obsession
Late night at the roastery. Cupping bowls lined up. Notebooks filled with scores. Sam stares at the board: origin names, roast profiles, tasting notes. Erases one line. Rewrites it. Steps back. It's never done.
Voiceover
Some people find the answer and stop looking.
We found the answer and kept going.
Great coffee starts
with a theory.
Scene 7 — The End Card
Slow fade to black. Hold for two beats. The Theory logo fades in — white on black. Simple. Clean. Below it: "Redding, California. Est. 2018." Hold. Then: "theorycoffee.com" fades in below. The piano resolves. Silence.
Production Notes
Duration
90 Seconds
Cut versions at :60, :30, and :15 for social and paid media.
Aspect Ratio
2.39:1 Cinematic
Wide letterbox. Crop to 16:9 for YouTube, 9:16 for stories, 1:1 for feed.
Color Grade
Warm, Desaturated
Lifted blacks. Copper highlights. Low contrast. Film-like. Think: Terrence Malick meets coffee.
Sound
Ambient + Piano
Foley-heavy: machine hum, bean crack, steam hiss. Piano enters Scene 3, builds through Scene 6.
Voiceover
Male, Mid-Register
Calm, unhurried. Not announcer-read. Sounds like a friend who takes coffee seriously.
Locations
3 Primary
Roastery (pre-dawn), California St. flagship (morning), origin farm footage (stock or trip).
Key Shots
01
Extreme Close-Up
Roaster dial turning. Steam rising. The machine waking up before dawn.
02
Wide + Aerial
Coffee farm hillside. Morning mist. Rows of cherry-laden trees stretching to the horizon.
03

Sam pulling a roast sample. Holding beans to the light. The decision moment.

Green beans cascading into the drum. 120fps. The transformation begins.

Following a barista from the grinder to the group head to the pour. One continuous take.
06

Customer's face in the moment of the first sip. Natural light from the cafe window.
07

Cupping table. Eight bowls in a row. Breaking the crust with a spoon.
08

Empty roastery at the end of the day. One light still on. The work continues.
The Manifesto

We believe coffee is a craft, not a commodity. That the farmer matters as much as the roaster. That a great cup should make you stop, even for a second, and think: this is different. We started on a backyard grill. We'll finish when there's nothing left to improve. We haven't found that point yet.
