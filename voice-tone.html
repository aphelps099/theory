Voice + Tone

How Theory sounds. The words we choose, the ones we don't, and why it matters.

The Principle

Write like you roast. Nothing extra.

Theory's voice is the verbal equivalent of its coffee: precise, confident, and free of filler. We don't over-explain. We don't use jargon to impress. We speak with the clarity of someone who knows their craft and respects your time.

Voice Attributes
Direct
Say it once. Say it clearly. If a sentence works without an adjective, cut the adjective.
Knowledgeable
We know coffee deeply. That knowledge shows through specificity, not lectures. Details over declarations.
Warm
Confident doesn't mean cold. We're inviting. The coffee shop is a gathering place. The writing should feel like one.
Grounded
We started on a backyard grill. That humility stays. No pretense. No posturing. The product speaks.

If you have to explain why the coffee is good, the coffee isn't good enough.

Brand Voice Principle
Tone Spectrum
Casual
Formal

Lean conversational, but never sloppy. We're the friend who happens to know a lot about coffee.

Playful
Serious

A spark of wit is welcome. But we're not performing. The craft is serious even when the vibe isn't.

Humble
Confident

Right in the middle. We earned the awards. But we still remember the backyard grill.

Technical
Accessible

Lean accessible. Use specific terms when they add value (Q-Grader, cupping score). Skip them when they don't.

The Rule

When in doubt, cut.

Every piece of Theory copy should pass one test: would Sam say this out loud? If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it. If it sounds like a person who loves coffee talking to another person who loves coffee, publish it.
