The Mark

The word is the logo. The period is the punctuation.

Theory doesn't use an icon. The wordmark carries full weight. Set in Jost 800, uppercase, tight tracking. The copper period is the only ornamentation. It signals completion. Confidence. A sentence that doesn't need to be longer.

Color Philosophy

Copper is the only color. Everything else is tone.

The palette runs on contrast: black, white, and copper. Copper does the work of warmth, craft, and attention. It appears on accent bars, hover states, and the period in the wordmark. It never competes with photography. It frames it.

Type Hierarchy

Sans leads. Serif punctuates.

Jost carries all headlines, navigation, labels, and body text. It's geometric, modern, and scales from 9px labels to 200px hero type without losing authority. Newsreader appears only in italics: pull quotes, emphasis words, and the tagline. It adds warmth without softening the tone.

Usage Guidelines
Do

Use the copper period on the wordmark at all sizes. It's not optional. It's the brand's only graphic mark.

Don't

Stack the wordmark over an icon or add graphic embellishments. The word is the mark. Nothing else.

Do

Maintain generous whitespace around the wordmark. Minimum clear space equals the height of the capital T.

Don't

Use copper as a background color for large areas. It's an accent. It earns attention by being used sparingly.
