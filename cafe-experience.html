Experience Principle
Service Touchpoints
The Door
Acknowledge every customer within 5 seconds. Eye contact and a greeting. The counter can wait. The person can't.
The Order
Know the menu cold. If a customer asks what's good, have a real answer. Not "everything." A specific bean. A specific drink. Lead with knowledge.
The Pull
Every espresso dialed in before doors open. If the shot isn't right, pull it again. The customer never sees a bad cup. That's the job.
The Handoff
Name on the cup. Eye contact on the handoff. The drink isn't done when it's made. It's done when someone smiles holding it.
The Space
Tables cleared within 2 minutes. Music at conversation level. Bathroom checked every hour. The room is the first impression and the last.
The Exit
Thank every customer who leaves. Not a script. A genuine acknowledgment. They chose us. That matters.

Eden Bakery. Every location.

Theory doesn't bake. Eden does. Fresh pastries delivered daily to every café. The partnership is visible, intentional, and non-negotiable. If it's in the case, it's from Eden. No exceptions.

Atmosphere Principles
Warm
Natural materials. Wood, concrete, copper fixtures. Nothing plastic. Nothing cheap. The space should feel built to last because it was.
Urban
Clean lines. Industrial lighting. The café is a neighborhood anchor, not a retreat. It belongs in the city, not in spite of it.
Focused
The coffee is the centerpiece. No visual clutter. Minimal signage. The menu board, the bar, and the cup. That's the hierarchy.
The Rule

If you wouldn't serve it to Sam, don't serve it.

Every decision in the café traces back to one filter: would the founder drink this, sit here, feel this? If the answer is no, fix it before anyone else notices.
