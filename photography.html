Photography

The images that carry the brand. How to shoot, treat, and use them.

California Street · Redding, CA
Direction

Shoot the real thing. Don't stage it.

Theory photography captures what's already happening. Natural light. Real customers. Actual baristas mid-pull. The images should feel observed, not directed. If it looks like a stock photo, reshoot it.

Mt. Shasta
Hilltop Drive
Mood

Warm. Urban. Unforced.

The overall feel is Northern California meets specialty coffee culture. Think morning light through café windows, steam rising from a pour-over, the texture of a weathered countertop. The atmosphere should invite you in without trying too hard.

Photo Treatments
Natural
Default treatment. Slightly desaturated, lifted contrast. Bright but not blown out.
saturate(0.85) contrast(1.05) brightness(1.05)
Muted
For background or overlay use. Pulled back saturation for text legibility.
saturate(0.5) contrast(1.1) brightness(0.95)
High Contrast
For hero moments. Punched-up contrast with natural color. Use sparingly.
saturate(0.9) contrast(1.2) brightness(1.0)

Shot Categories
01
Space + Architecture
Café interiors, exteriors, signage, details. Capture the physical brand. Show the materials, the light, the layout.
02
Process + Craft
Roasting, cupping, pulling shots, latte art. Hands at work. The tools and rituals that define the trade.
03
Product + Detail
Bags, beans, cups, packaging. Shot clean against natural surfaces. No props that don't belong in a café.
04
People + Community
Baristas, regulars, events. Candid over posed. Real expressions, real moments. No one looks at the camera.
Eden Bakery
Remember
The best Theory photo is one you'd want framed on the wall of the café.
Guidelines
Do

Shoot in natural light. Morning golden hour and overcast days produce the best Theory images.

Don't

Use flash or artificial key lights. The images should feel found, not produced.

Do

Include environmental context. A cup on a countertop is better than a cup on a white seamless.

Don't

Over-saturate or over-filter. The coffee is the star. The edit should disappear.
