Digital + Social

The screen is a storefront. Every post, page, and pixel should feel like walking into Theory.

The Principle

Post less. Mean more.

Theory doesn't chase algorithms. We publish when we have something worth saying. Quality over frequency. Every piece of content should earn the attention it asks for.

Website Standards
01
Typography First
Jost for navigation, headings, and UI. Newsreader for editorial moments. No decorative fonts. Let whitespace do the heavy lifting.
02
Photography Rules
Natural light. Muted tones. No stock. Every image on-site should be shot in a Theory location or of Theory product. Chapter 04 filters apply.
03
Ecommerce UX
Clean product pages. One hero image, tasting notes, origin story. No clutter. The checkout should feel as considered as the pour.
Content Pillars
01
Process
Behind the roast. Sourcing trips, cupping sessions, new arrivals. Show the craft without over-explaining it.
40% of content
02
Product
New releases, seasonal rotations, restocks. Clean product shots. Let the bag and the bean speak.
25% of content
03
Place
The cafés, the community, the people. Redding, Shasta, the culture around the cup. Real moments, not staged ones.
25% of content
04
Point of View
Opinion pieces, industry takes, the Theory perspective on coffee culture. Direct and grounded, never preachy.
10% of content

Don't post because it's Tuesday. Post because it matters.

Content Principle
Digital Guidelines
Do

Respond to comments and DMs within 24 hours. Be warm, be brief. Sound like a person, not a brand deck.

Don't

Use trending audio, memes, or formats that don't align with the brand. If it could be any coffee shop, it's not Theory.

Do

Maintain a consistent color temperature across the feed. Edit with Chapter 04 photo treatments. Warm, slightly desaturated, natural.

Don't

Over-hashtag. Five maximum per post. No hashtag walls. No #coffee #coffeelover #coffeetime #coffeeoftheday. Be specific or skip it.

The Standard

Would you follow this account? Be honest.

Before you publish anything, ask: would this make someone stop scrolling? If the answer is no, it's not ready. Every post is a first impression for someone new.
